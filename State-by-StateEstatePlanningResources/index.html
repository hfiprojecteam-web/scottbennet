State-by-State Estate Planning Resources

The Funded Trust & Estate Planning Protocol

Scott Bennet · 2026 · For use with the 90-Day Funded Trust Protocol

This reference page gives you direct links to the official resources you need to fund your living trust: county deed recorders, state-specific transfer rules, and the beneficiary designation portals at Vanguard, Fidelity, and Schwab. All links lead to official government or institutional websites. Bookmark this page and return to it as you work through each phase of the 90-Day FTP.

NATIONAL RESOURCES — All States

Vanguard — Beneficiary Designations

Update IRA Beneficiaries at Vanguard → Vanguard Account Services Contact →
Navigate: My Profile → Beneficiaries. For 401k plans through Vanguard, contact your plan administrator.

Fidelity — Beneficiary Designations

Update Beneficiaries at Fidelity → Fidelity Customer Service →
Navigate: Accounts & Trade → Beneficiaries. For employer 401k, contact your HR plan admin — Fidelity acts as record-keeper only.

Charles Schwab — Beneficiary Designations

Update Beneficiaries at Schwab → Schwab Estate Planning Services →
Navigate: Service → Beneficiary. Schwab has a dedicated estate planning documentation team — ask for them directly.

TreasuryDirect — Savings Bonds

TreasuryDirect.gov → Manage Your TreasuryDirect Account →
Log in → ManageDirect → Update Secondary Owner or Beneficiary. Tip: secondary owner designation allows automatic transfer at death without probate.

IRS — Trust Tax ID (EIN)

Apply for Trust EIN Online → Form 1041 (Trust Income Tax Return) →
Your successor trustee will need an EIN for the trust after your death. Takes ~5 minutes online. Required before opening a trust checking account.

RUFADAA — Digital Asset Authority

Uniform Law Commission — RUFADAA →
Adopted in 49 states. Gives your successor trustee legal authority to access digital accounts. Check your state's adoption status and any state-specific modifications below.

Social Security Administration

SSA Contact & Local Offices → Survivors Benefits Information →
Notify SSA immediately upon death. Benefits paid after date of death must be returned. Call 1-800-772-1213.

Digital Legacy — Platform Settings

Apple Digital Legacy Contact → Google Inactive Account Manager → Meta/Facebook Legacy Contact →
Set these up now — before you need them. Access keys should be stored in your fireproof box, not in cloud storage.

STATE-SPECIFIC RESOURCES — Deed Recording, Homestead & Transfer Rules

California

County Deed Recording

Find Your County Recorder → Santa Clara County Recorder → Los Angeles County Recorder →
Recording fees vary by county. Most counties accept walk-in recording. California requires a Preliminary Change of Ownership Report (PCOR) when recording a deed.

Homestead Exemption

CA Board of Equalization — Exemptions →
California's automatic homestead exemption ($300,000–$600,000 depending on county median) is not affected by transfer to a revocable trust. No re-filing required.

Transfer Tax & Notes

Transfers to your own revocable trust are generally exempt from documentary transfer tax under Revenue & Tax Code §11930. Confirm with your county recorder. Prop 19 (2021) affects parent-child transfers — consult your attorney if conveying property to a trust that will benefit children.

Texas

County Deed Recording

Texas County Clerks Directory → Harris County (Houston) Clerk → Dallas County Clerk →
Texas deed recording is handled by County Clerks (not Recorders). Fees are typically $25–$40 for the first page plus $4 per additional page.

Homestead Exemption

Texas Comptroller — Homestead Exemption →
Texas homestead exemption is preserved when property is transferred to a revocable trust. You may need to re-file the homestead exemption form with your county appraisal district after the transfer — confirm with your county.

Transfer Tax & Notes

Texas has no state deed transfer tax. No documentary stamps required. Transfer of homestead to revocable trust does not trigger due-on-sale clause under Garn–St. Germain (confirm with mortgage servicer).

Florida

County Deed Recording

MyFloridaCounty.com — All County Clerks → Miami-Dade County Clerk → Orange County (Orlando) Clerk →
Florida documentary stamp tax: $0.70 per $100 of consideration (Miami-Dade: $0.60 + surtax). Transfers to revocable trusts are typically exempt — cite §201.02(7)(b), Fla. Stat. Your attorney should note this on the deed.

Homestead Exemption — CRITICAL

Florida Department of Revenue — Exemptions →
⚠ Florida homestead exemption CAN be lost if the trust is not properly structured. The trust must meet specific requirements under §196.041, Fla. Stat. Have your attorney confirm homestead status before and after the transfer. File Form DR-501 with your county property appraiser if required.

Elective Share & Notes

Florida has a statutory elective share (30%) for surviving spouses. Assets in a revocable trust are generally included in the elective share calculation. Confirm with your estate planning attorney if you have a spouse.

New York

County Deed Recording

NY County Clerks Directory → NYC ACRIS — Property Records → Westchester County Clerk →
New York City has five borough county clerks. ACRIS handles online NYC property record searches. Recording fees vary significantly by county.

Transfer Tax

NY Real Property Transfer Tax: $4 per $500 of value. NYC adds additional mansion tax for properties over $1M. Transfers to revocable trusts may be exempt — confirm with your attorney. RP-5217 form required in most counties.

STAR Exemption & Notes

NY STAR School Tax Relief →
Transfer to revocable trust generally does not affect STAR exemption eligibility if you remain a beneficial owner and primary resident. Notify your local assessor's office after the transfer to confirm.

Washington

County Deed Recording

Washington County Directory → King County (Seattle) Recordings →
Washington recording fees: $203.50 for standard documents. REET (Real Estate Excise Tax) applies unless exempt. Transfers to your own revocable trust are exempt under WAC 458-61A-211.

Notes

Washington has no income tax but does have an estate tax (threshold: $2.193M in 2026). Assets in a revocable trust are included in the taxable estate. Consult a Washington estate attorney if your estate may approach this threshold.

Colorado

County Clerk & Recorder

Colorado County Clerks & Recorders → Denver County Assessor →
Colorado uses a Clerk & Recorder system. Filing fees: approximately $13 for first page + $5 per additional page. No state real estate transfer tax.

Notes

Colorado has no state estate or inheritance tax. Homestead exemption ($75,000; $105,000 if elderly/disabled) is not affected by transfer to revocable trust — no re-filing required.

Arizona

County Recorder

Arizona County Recorders → Maricopa County (Phoenix) Recorder → Pima County (Tucson) Recorder →
Arizona: no real estate transfer tax. Affidavit of Property Value required for transfers — available at the county recorder's office. Revocable trust transfers are typically marked "not a sale."

Illinois

County Recorder

Illinois County Officials Directory → Cook County (Chicago) Assessor →
Illinois Real Estate Transfer Tax: $0.50 per $500 state + county/municipal taxes. Chicago has additional city transfer tax. Transfers to revocable trusts where consideration is less than $100 may be exempt — confirm with your county recorder.

Notes

Illinois has a state estate tax (threshold: $4M). Revocable trust assets are included in the taxable estate. Illinois does not have the stepped-up basis rules modification — no adverse income tax impact from trust transfer.

Pennsylvania

County Deed Recording

Pennsylvania County Directory → Philadelphia Department of Records →
PA Realty Transfer Tax: 1% state + 1% local = 2% total (Philadelphia adds additional). Transfers to revocable trusts where grantor is the beneficiary are typically exempt under 72 P.S. §8102-C.3(6). Note this exemption on the deed.

Georgia

County Deed Recording

Georgia Clerk of Superior Court Directory → Georgia Clerks Authority — Online Search →
Georgia deed recording is through the Clerk of the Superior Court (not County Clerk). Intangible recording tax applies to mortgages, not deeds. No state real estate transfer tax on deed transfers to revocable trusts.

North Carolina

County Register of Deeds

NC Registers of Deeds → Wake County (Raleigh) Register of Deeds →
NC Excise Tax (deed stamps): $2 per $1,000 of consideration. Transfers to revocable trusts for $0 consideration: exempt from excise tax. File with Register of Deeds in the county where property is located.

Virginia

Circuit Court Clerk — Land Records

Virginia Circuit Court Clerks → Virginia Land Records — Online Search →
Virginia deed recording is through Circuit Court Clerks. Recordation tax: $0.25 per $100. Grantor tax: $0.50 per $500. Transfers to revocable trusts may qualify for exemptions — cite Va. Code §58.1-811. Confirm with your Circuit Court Clerk.

Nevada

County Recorder

Nevada County Services → Clark County (Las Vegas) Recorder →
Nevada: no real estate transfer tax. No state income tax. No state estate tax. Deed recording fees: approximately $25 for first page. One of the most trust-friendly states — Nevada Trust Code is highly favorable for asset protection.

Oregon

County Recorder

Oregon County Offices → Multnomah County (Portland) Recording →
Oregon: no real estate transfer tax (except a few cities). Washington County and Multnomah County have local transfer taxes — confirm before recording. Deed recording fee: ~$96 + $5/page over 5 pages.

Michigan

County Register of Deeds

Michigan Register of Deeds → Wayne County (Detroit) Register of Deeds →
Michigan Transfer Tax: state $3.75 per $500 + county $0.55 per $500. Transfers to revocable trusts are exempt from state transfer tax under MCL §207.526(b). File an Affidavit of Exempt Real Property Transfer (Form L-4260).

New Jersey

County Clerk

NJ County Clerks Directory →
NJ Realty Transfer Fee: based on sales price (ranges from 1% to 1.45% for properties over $1M). Transfers to revocable trusts where consideration is $0 are typically minimal fee. NJ has both estate tax (for estates over $2M) and inheritance tax — consult your attorney.

Minnesota

County Recorder

Minnesota County Recorders → Hennepin County (Minneapolis) Recording →
Minnesota Deed Tax: $1.65 per $500 of value. Transfers to revocable trusts may be subject to deed tax — consult your attorney. MN has a state estate tax (threshold: $3M). Homestead: re-filing may be required after transfer.

Tennessee

County Register of Deeds

Tennessee Registers of Deeds → Shelby County (Memphis) Register of Deeds →
Tennessee Documentary Transfer Tax: $0.37 per $100. Transfers to revocable trusts are generally exempt — cite TCA §67-4-409. No state income tax or estate tax. One of the most trust-friendly states for estate planning.

Maryland

Circuit Court Land Records

Maryland Land Records — Online Search →
Maryland Transfer Tax: state 0.5% + county (varies 0-1.5%). Transfers to revocable trusts are typically exempt. MD has a state estate tax (threshold: $5M) and inheritance tax. Consult your attorney for tax-efficient titling strategies.

Massachusetts

Registry of Deeds

Massachusetts Registries of Deeds → MassLandRecords.com — Online Search →
Massachusetts Excise Tax (deeds): $2.28 per $500. Transfers to revocable trusts: generally exempt if no consideration. MA has a state estate tax with a $2M exemption — one of the lowest in the US. Critical planning state for larger estates.
No specific resources found for this state. Use the national resources above and contact your county recorder or register of deeds directly.
